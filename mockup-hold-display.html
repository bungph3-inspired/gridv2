3-leg compound — your effective edge required to break even is ~10.9%, even though each leg averages 3.7% hold.

Compounded hold math: 1 − ∏(1 − legHold). Three legs at 2.1% / 2.3% / 6.8% → ~10.9% slip hold. None of DK/FD/MGM show this; Pinnacle doesn't either. It's a uniquely sharp/educational touch and would be a real differentiator for GridV2.

My take

Ship Variant A + Slip-Level Hold. Variant A reads cleanly in the existing column header without breaking the board layout, and one hold number per market (per league) is honest — that's how books actually price internally. Variant B is the second-best add-on once you start tiering hold by game (live in-game markets, etc.). Variant C is fun to build but the chip clutters cells and the number is redundant within a two-sided market.

The slip-level hold callout is the move that would make GridV2 feel obviously sharper than any commercial book — Pinnacle doesn't even do it. Pairs naturally with reduced-juice main lines.
